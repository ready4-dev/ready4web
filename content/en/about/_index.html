The goal of ready4 is to implement a transparent, reusable and updatable computational model of youth mental health.

{{< /blocks/cover >}} {{% blocks/lead %}}

Catalyst

In July 2017, with funding support from VicHealth, Orygen and Victoria University, we began to develop a prototype software framework for implementing an open source computational model of resilience in young people. At that time, the project was called readyforwhatsnext.

{{% /blocks/lead %}} {{% blocks/lead color="white"%}}

Minimum Viable Product

Currently, we aim to develop and release an initial set of modules for a more extensive economic model, maintain and improve that model and then demonstrate its practical utility for generating useful insights for youth mental health decision-makers. We are pursuing these goals as multiple independently funded and governed modelling projects at Orygen and by an Australian Government Research Training Program (RTP) Scholarship funded PhD project at Monash University Health Economics Group.

{{% /blocks/lead %}} {{% blocks/lead color="primary"%}}

Adoption

With additional help, we hope to be able to progress longer term goals to build a community of users and contributors, extend the ready4 model and integrate it with other open source tools.
