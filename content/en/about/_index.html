The goal of ready4 is to be a valid and useful youth mental health systems model.

{{< /blocks/cover >}} {{% blocks/lead %}}

ready4 was catalysed in July 2017 with funding from VicHealth, Orygen and Victoria University to develop an open source simulation model of resilience in young people. At that time, the project was called readyforwhatsnext.

{{% /blocks/lead %}} {{< blocks/section >}} {{% blocks/feature icon="fas fa-comment" title="Stakeholder Engagement" %}} We got [advice and feedback](/docs/contribution-guidelines/contribution-types/advisory/) from young people, policy professionals, [modellers](/docs/getting-started/users/modeller/) and other researchers. {{% /blocks/feature %}} {{% blocks/feature icon="fas fa-map-marked" title="Conceptual Model" %}} We developed a [conceptual model](/blog/2021/02/12/readyforwhatsnext-conference-posters-now-available/) to inform the development of the computational model. {{% /blocks/feature %}} {{% blocks/feature icon="fas fa-check" title="Prototype" %}} We authored [development release software](/docs/getting-started/software/status/development-releases/) to support implementation of the ready4 [framework](/docs/framework/) and [model](/docs/model/). {{% /blocks/feature %}} {{< /blocks/section >}} {{% blocks/lead %}}

The current phase of development of ready4 is focused on achieving Minimum Viable Product status and is being progressed by a PhD project at Monash University's Health Economics Group and multiple modelling projects at Orygen's Health Services and Outcomes Research team.

{{% /blocks/lead %}} {{< blocks/section >}} {{< cardpane >}} {{< card header="PhD" footer="Primary Supervisor: Cathy Mihalopoulos">}} The PhD candidature of ready4's project lead Matthew Hamilton is focused on developing the ready4 framework and demonstrating its practical utility for implementing valid and useful tools to inform policymaking and system design relating to primary mental health services for young people in Australia. The PhD is supported by an Australian Government Research Training Program (RTP) Scholarship and, in addition to peer-reviewed publications, aims to produce outputs including production releases of framework libraries and a core set of module libraries, open access datasets, reproducible analyses and decision aids. {{< /card >}} {{< card header="Modelling projects" footer="Head of Unit: Sue Cotton">}} A number of modelling projects at Orygen, apply, extend and/or supplement the core ready4 modules being developed under the PhD program. Examples of current and recent projects include those in choice modelling and utility mapping. Each project is independently funded and governed. Details of project funding are available in the citation information, meta-data and acknowledgements of the module libraries, datasets, analyses and manuscripts associated with each project. {{< /card >}} {{< /cardpane >}} {{< /blocks/section >}} {{< blocks/section >}}

All ready4 software to date has been developed by Matthew Hamilton, Glen Weisner and Caroline Gao. Our planned future phase of development will therefore focus on building a community to sustain, extend and apply ready4 - and we need your help to make this happen!
